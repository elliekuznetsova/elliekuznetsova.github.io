About me

Hi there! My name is Elizaveta Kuznetsova [yeh-lee-zah-VYEH-tah kooz-NYET-soh-vah]. In media and publications, you’ll mostly see my legal name, Elizaveta Kuznetsova, but in casual contexts, people usually refer to me as “Ellie”. As a rule of thumb, when in doubt (and we’re not talking legal documents), go with Ellie — you can’t go wrong with that.

I received a BA in Linguistics from the Department of Foreign Languages at Pushkin Leningrad State University, followed by an MA in Applied Linguistics and Teaching English as a Second Language from the English Department at Northern Arizona University. My bachelor's program was focused on interpretation and translation studies. During my MA, I dove into research methods, corpus linguistics, and teaching; I worked with instructor feedback and English composition research, looking into higher and lower order concerns in instructor comments and student–instructor rapport building. We're in the process of finalizing publication of the Writing Feedback Corpus (WFC) related to the instructor feedback research, with the ultimate goal of ______________.

I am currently finishing my MS in Computational Social Science at UC San Diego, combining my interests in linguistics, data, and language models. My research focuses on bilingualism, code-switching, natural language processing, corpus linguistics, and second language acquisition. In the capstone porject, we attempt to expand speech technology to low-resource languages like Tira, which have very limited available data. By fine-tuning the XLSR model for keyword search, we support linguistic preservation and improve accessibility for underrepresented speech communities such as those in South Kordofan, Sudan.
