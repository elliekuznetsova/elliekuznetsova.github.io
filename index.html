About me

Hi there! My name is Elizaveta Kuznetsova [eh-lee-zah-VYEH-tah kooz-NYET-soh-vah] (happy to hear feedback on my phonetic spelling!), but I casually go by “Ellie.” In media and publications, you’ll mostly see my legal name, Elizaveta Kuznetsova. In real life, though, I usually introduce myself as Ellie. My Russian-speaking friends call me Liza (which I know makes things even more confusing for you). For them, it feels natural because for the first 25 years of my life, I was always Liza. These days, I prefer “Ellie” whenever possible. This isn’t always an option for publications, so you’ll still see “Elizaveta” in academic contexts. As a rule of thumb, when in doubt (and we’re not talking legal documents), go with Ellie — you can’t go wrong with that.

Previously, I have worked with instructor feedback and English composition research, looking into higher and lower order concerns in instructor comments and student–instructor rapport building. We're planning to publish the Writing Feedback Corpus (WFC) related to the instructor feedback research soon. Before going to UC San Diego, I received an MA in Applied Linguistics and Teaching English as a Second Language from the English Department at Northern Arizona University and BA in Linguistics from the Department of Foreign Languages at Pushkin Leningrad State University. My bachelor's program was focus on interpretation and translation studies, while my MA has dived into research methods, corpus linguistics, and teaching.

Right now I’m an M.S. student in Computational Social Science at UC San Diego, combining my interests in language, data, and human communication. My research focuses on bilingualism, code-switching, natural language processing, corpus linguistics, cognitive linguistics, psycholinguistics, and second language acquisition.
