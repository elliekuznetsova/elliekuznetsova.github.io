About me

Hi there! My name is Elizaveta Kunetsova [eh-lee-zah-VYEH-tah kooz-NYET-soh-vah] (happy to hear feedback on my phonetic spelling) but I casually go by "Ellie". In media and other publications, you might mostly see me under my legal name Elizaveta Kuznetsova, however, if I meet people n real life I usually introduce myself as Ellie. My Russian-speaking friends call me Liza, which makes things even more confusing, I know. For them, it might feel unnatural to call me by my chosen American name, especially considering that for whole 25 years of my life I have always been Liza. Right now, things changed and I prefer name "Ellie" whenever possible. This is not possible for publications, so you will occasionally see my name as Elizaveta. To sum up, whenver in doubt, and we're not talking legal things, stick to Ellie - you can't wrong with that. I’m an M.S. student in Computational Social Science at UC San Diego, combining my interests in language, data, and human communication. My research focuses on bilingualism, code-switching, natural language processing, corpus linguistics, cognitive linguistics, psycholinguistics, and second language acquisition.
