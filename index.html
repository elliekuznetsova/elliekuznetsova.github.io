Elizaveta “Ellie” Kuznetsova

M.S. Computational Social Science, UC San Diego (Expected June 2026)

I am a graduate student researcher at UC San Diego working at the intersection of bilingualism, phonetics, and natural language processing. My research combines corpus linguistics, experimental methods, and machine learning to study multilingual language use and evaluation.

Affiliations

  • Bilingualism in Context Lab (PI: Anne Beatty-Martinez)
  • Phonetics Lab (PI: Mark Simmons; Faculty Advisor: Will Styler)

My work includes evaluation of multilingual LLMs, code-switched corpora, low-resource ASR, and dialogue-based reference tasks.

Research Experience

Graduate Student Researcher — Bilingualism in Context Lab, UC San Diego | Sep 2025 – Present

  • Develop end-to-end evaluation pipelines for code-switched minimal pairs to assess LLM grammatical knowledge
  • Design coding schemes and annotation guidelines for human judgment tasks
  • Evaluate XLM-RoBERTa on Spanish–English code-switched data
  • Compare multilingual embeddings (FastText) and visualize representations with t-SNE
  • Analyze utterance length reduction in repeated reference tasks

Graduate Student Researcher — Phonetics Lab, UC San Diego | Sep 2025 – Present

  • Train and fine-tune XLSR acoustic models for low-resource speech recognition
  • Evaluate ASR outputs against human transcriptions
  • Analyze systematic error patterns
  • Build and optimize Pynini-based letter-to-phone mappings

Principal Investigator (Independent) — UC San Diego & Northern Arizona University | Apr 2022 – Oct 2025

  • Directed end-to-end research projects and coordinated research teams
  • Built and annotated the Writing Feedback Corpus (WFC)
  • Authored major sections of peer-reviewed manuscripts
